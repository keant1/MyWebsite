About Me

I’m a third-year undergraduate student studying Engineering Physics and Management at McMaster University. My passions include science & technology, innovation, community-building and sharing ingenuity.

As an avid life-long learner, I seek to improve my technical prowess to foster positive change and develop smart systems capable to drive us towards sustainable growth. With numerous projects under my belt and extensive experience working with teams, I am ready to adapt to any situation.

Some of my hobbies include listening to spoken word poetry, finding new podcasts on topics ranging from global economics to science fiction, reading old novels and tracking new technologies through projects on hackster.io.

Future Work

Personal projects currently in progress.

Miss Moneypenny

I am currently developing an algorithmic trading bot for cryptocurrencies to learn more about data science, web development, interfacing with APIs, statistics and modern technologies. Cryptocurrency was chosen as the medium due to the wealth of user support in documentation, open-source code and online forums as well as the high accessibility of cryptocurrency.

Tools: Python. Python libraries: Pandas, MatPlotLib, Numpy, SciKitLearn

Starfire

A multicoloured LED strip capable of being wirelessly controlled from both Amazon Alexa and Apple Siri devices. Although many devices like this already exist on the market, I had a hard time finding LED strips that would sync with both Apple and Amazon devices. Furthermore, I’ll learn more by making one myself and seeing how the technology works rather than using a commercial solution.

Tools: C, Javascript, NodeJS Homebriddge, Rapsberry Pi, ESP32 Microcontroller, Amazon Alexa, Siri, MQTT communication protocol

McMaster Engineering Welcome Week

Welcome Week Planning Committee

This year, I am part of the planning committee to develop events adaptive to a diverse group of incoming first-year students to foster a culture of inclusion and mentorship. Through planning Welcome Week, I’m acting as a community builder, communicating with multiple stakeholders to coordinate leadership and logistics for over 1000 first year students and 150 Engineering representatives.

Welcome Week Representative

As an Engineering Representative for Welcome Week 2018 and 2019, I worked to promote a culture of diversity and foster community for incoming first year students. As a Welcome Week rep, I developed mentorship and leadership skills working with first years, while balancing ensuring student engagement and comfort. This was done by leading events in a fast-paced environment as first year students began to call McMaster home.
